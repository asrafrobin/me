On the Bank of Feni River

Feni River is a river in southeastern Bangladesh and Tripura state of India. 116 km long Feni river originates in South Tripura district and flows through Sabroom town and then enters Bangladesh.
This part of the river I've framed is known as Suvopur. The further west of the river separates Chittagong from Feni district

A Temple Over the Cloud : Marayontong

Marayontong, locally known as (মেরাইতং জাদী) is a 1640ft. high peak with a sacred Buddhist temple- located in the heart of Alikodom, Bandarban. The best thing about this particular journey was, we trekked the hill at night under a sky full of stars! I must admit it was the hardest part of the journey, but trust me your labour will paid off once you reach to the peak.

A Floating Paradise : Nijhum Island

Nijhum Dwip (নিঝুম দ্বীপ) is a small island in the deepest of Noakhali. This virgin new island of Nijhum Dwip raised on the estuary of the great Meghna channel in the mouth of The Bay of Bengal. The geographical location has been identified just 2 kilometers south west of Hatia under Noakhali district. This newly accredited island may be called as cluster of islands consist of more than 4-5 small islands like Char Osman, Char Kamla, Corner of Char Osman and isolated from the mainland by Hatia channel. It’s a natural beauty of mangrove forests where you will find Dears of specific species and Monkeys. A huge numbers of migrated birds come in this Island in winter season, enhancing the beauty of this Island. The communication is not excellent but if some one wishes to bear the trouble to go to this Island, he will be enchant to see the beauty of this Island. The locals seemed less-friendly to the tourists.

The Fountain of Youth : Dhuppani

No, It is not the mythical fountain of youth which can restore your youth, but it will defenitely restore the calm of your mind. Dhuppani waterfall (ধূপপানি ঝর্ণা) is in the deepest of the Bilaichori, Rangamati which is actually a scared place for Buddhist. No wonder why the monks choose this place for their meditation. Total silence except the roar of the falling water.
Our journey started from Kaptai that took approx. 3/4 hours to reach Bilachori, a small union of Rangamati. The best thing about Bilaichori is the food!. You can actually choose your meal in advance and when you return for dinner, you will taste something unearthy! It takes around 2 hours to reach from Bilachori by boat, and then around 2/2.5 hours of trekking before you reach the mighty Dhuppani. Dhuppani shows its mightyness in the rainy season that couldn't manage. So there wasn't that much of water at that time but the beauty of kaptai lake from the boat on a full moon is something one can never forget.
